Death




The Book Thief is a novel with a unique narrative style. After all, the narrator in The Book Thief is Death. Yes, Death. Death is omnipresent, watching but not interfering in the lives of those caught in the turmoil of Nazi Germany. It's a unique perspective that not only sets the tone for the novel but also invites readers to consider the fragility of life and the inevitability of death. Death is characterized as caring too much and not at all at the same time, which works so well with the story as a whole.
